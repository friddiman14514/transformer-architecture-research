# Timeline of Transformer Evolution (2017‑Present)

| Year | Milestone | Key Contributions |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **2017** | *Attention Is All You Need* (Vaswani et al.) | Introduced the Transformer architecture, self‑attention, multi‑head attention, positional encodings. |
| **2018** | BERT (Devlin et al.) | Demonstrated the power of pre‑training Transformer encoders for downstream NLP tasks. |
| **2018** | GPT (Radford et al.) | Showed effective language generation with a decoder‑only Transformer. |
| **2019** | GPT‑2 (Radford et al.) | Scaled up model size, zero‑shot learning capabilities. |
| **2020** | Vision Transformer (Dosovitskiy et al.) | Applied pure Transformer architecture to image classification. |
| **2020** | T5 (Raffel et al.) | Unified text‑to‑text framework using encoder‑decoder Transformers. |
| **2021** | Longformer, Big Bird, Performer | Introduced sparse and linear‑time attention mechanisms for longer sequences. |
| **2022** | Switch Transformers, GLaM | Explored mixture‑of‑experts to scale model capacity efficiently. |
| **2023** | ChatGPT (OpenAI) | Commercialized large‑scale conversational models based on decoder Transformers. |
| **2023‑2024** | Retrieval‑Augmented Generation, Multimodal Transformers | Integrated external knowledge sources and multi‑modality (text, image, audio). |
| **2025‑Present** | Efficient Transformers (e.g., FlashAttention) | Optimized attention kernels for GPU/TPU, reducing memory footprint and latency. |

The Transformer has become a universal building block across NLP, computer vision, speech, and multimodal AI, spawning a vast ecosystem of variants and applications.
